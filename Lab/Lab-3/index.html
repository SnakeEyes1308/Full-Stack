Famous Personalities

Albert Einstein

German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity.

Notable Work: Theory of Relativity

Einstein's work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.

Marie Curie

Polish and naturalized-French physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity.

Notable Achievements: Nobel Prizes in Physics & Chemistry

The first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win in multiple sciences.

Leonardo da Vinci

Italian polymath of the Renaissance known for his contributions to art, science, and engineering.

Masterpieces: Mona Lisa & The Last Supper

His works and journals influenced countless fields, demonstrating a uniquely curious and inventive mind.

Isaac Newton

English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, and author who is widely recognised as one of the most influential scientists of all time.

Key Contributions: Laws of Motion & Universal Gravitation

Newton made groundbreaking contributions to mathematics, optics, and physics, laying the foundation for classical mechanics.

Nikola Tesla

Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

Innovations: AC Power & The Tesla Coil

Tesla's work formed the basis of wireless communication and the electrical power grid.

Ada Lovelace

English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Pioneer of Computing

Lovelace's notes on the Analytical Engine include what is essentially the first algorithm intended to be processed by a machine, making her one of the first computer programmers.
